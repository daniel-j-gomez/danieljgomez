Research Interests: I am fascinated by the structural biology of diverse biological systems, including human mitochondria and exosomes (EVs), and their potential as targets for patient-specific therapeutics. Additionally, I am intrigued by using plant vesicle tracking and bacterial membrane self-assembly to develop novel drug delivery systems. Project II: Structural and Immunological Characterization of Viral Infections and Cancer
Our project focuses on the structural and immunological characterization of viral infections and their link to cancer development. Using cutting-edge techniques in structural molecular biology, we aim to elucidate the structure of the HTLV-1 intasome and develop structure-based vaccine design for immunotherapy against infectious diseases and cancer. We also investigate the role of host-pathogen interactions in co-infection and cancer, with a focus on the interactions between dendritic cells and oncogenic viruses such as HIV-1 and HTLV-1. Additionally, we explore the potential of ribozymes as precision weapons for gene therapy in incurable diseases. Our research involves the use of in-person and virtual presentations, publications, and poster presentations to disseminate our findings and contribute to the field of biology, biochemistry, microbiology, and immunology.
